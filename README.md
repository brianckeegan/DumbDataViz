# DumbDataViz
Examples of how to visualize data dumbly.

On December 14, the National Review Online [tweeted](https://twitter.com/NRO/status/676516015078039556) the following chart plotting annual global temperature over time.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/nro_tweet.PNG" alt="National Review Online tweet" width="600px">

Of course blowing out the y-axis to show the temperature scale from 0-100 obscures the underlying trend, which shows a significant increase in average temperatures over the past century:

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/Fig.A2.gif" alt="Official NASA temperature data" width="400px">

Building off of Sean McElwee's appropriate [response](https://twitter.com/SeanMcElwee/status/676521088105496577), let's have some fun at the expense of NRO's inept data visualization choices using basic economic data.

Unemployment has been flat for decades!

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/unemployment.png" alt="DumbDataViz unemployment" width="400px">

Unfortunately, US GDP hasn't grown despite constant employment levels.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/gdp.png" alt="DumbDataViz gdp" width="400px">

This chart *proves* that the federal debt isn't actually growing.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/debt.png" alt="DumbDataViz debt" width="400px">

It's remarkable how consistent inflation has been over this whole timeframe.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/cpi.png" alt="DumbDataViz Consumer Price Index" width="400px">

Treasury yields are very stable over time.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/treasury10.png" alt="DumbDataViz Treasury" width="400px">

Market volatility is rare and minor.

<img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianckeegan/DumbDataViz/master/vix.png" alt="DumbDataViz vix" width="400px">

Equity prices are generally stable with consistent growth.
